About

I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering at NYU Tandon School of Engineering, working in the Computational Psychiatry Lab at NYU Grossman School of Medicine under the mentorship of Dr. Katharina Schultebraucks.

My research focuses on developing multimodal AI systems for scalable psychiatric assessment. I build tools that analyze clinical interview recordings, integrating vocal, facial, and linguistic signals, to detect mental health risk with interpretable, clinician-understandable reasoning. My work spans two NIH-funded studies on healthcare worker burnout (R01HL156134) and PTSD prediction in trauma survivors (R01MH129856).

Before starting my PhD, I completed my M.S. in Data Science and Analytics at Montclair State University (GPA: 3.7/4.0) and worked as a Data Science Intern at NYU Grossman School of Medicine, where I built computational pipelines for speech phenotyping in clinical populations.

Research

Multimodal Digital Phenotyping

Designing and deploying audio-visual-linguistic feature extraction pipelines for video-recorded clinical interviews. Processing 650+ clinical assessments across 39 features and 5 modalities (biological, audio, video, psychological, physiological) with 97.3% data completeness on NYU's HPC cluster.

LLM-Based Psychiatric Assessment

Co-developing multi-model LLM frameworks that perform clinically grounded reasoning over trauma narratives and clinical interviews to predict psychiatric outcomes such as PTSD and occupational burnout, with interpretable chain-of-thought rationale.

Biological Stress Phenotyping

Using Latent Profile Analysis on cortisol, inflammatory cytokines, and psychological scales to identify distinct burnout phenotypes in healthcare workers, revealing clinically meaningful subgroups not captured by self-report alone.

Fairness and Equity in Clinical AI

Evaluating AI-based screening tools across demographic groups to identify and mitigate disparities, informed by our lab's published framework on cultural integration and digital inclusivity in LLM deployment for mental health.
